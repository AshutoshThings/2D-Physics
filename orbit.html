🌍 Orbit Simulation

🔬 Physics Behind the Simulation

🌍 Newtonian Gravity

Every object with mass attracts every other object. In our case, the orbiting body is attracted toward a central mass (like a planet or star).

Equation: F = G × M × m / r²

This results in acceleration:
a = F / m = G × M / r²

In vector form:
𝐚⃗ = − (G × M) / |𝐫⃗|³ × 𝐫⃗

⚙️ Numerical Methods

Simulations use step-by-step calculations to update motion using integration methods.

Euler’s Method

v(t+Δt) = v(t) + a(t) × Δt
r(t+Δt) = r(t) + v(t) × Δt

✅ Fast but error-prone over time

Runge-Kutta (RK4)

More accurate: takes 4 slope samples per step.

✅ Ideal for curved paths like orbits

🔢 Constants Used

  • G (Gravitational Constant, scaled): 5000
  • M (Central Mass): 100
  • Time Step (Δt): 1/60 sec
  • Initial Orbit Radius: 100 px
  • Initial Velocity: v = √(G × M / r)
